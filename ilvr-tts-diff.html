Zero Shot Voice Conditioning for Denoising Diffusion TTS Models

Alon Levkovitch, Eliya Nachmani, Lior Wolf

This post presents "Zero Shot Voice Conditioning for Denoising Diffusion TTS Models", a method for speech synthesis from unseen speakers using only a single reference with no training.

We present a novel way for conditioning a pretrained denoising diffusion speech model to produce speech in the voice of a novel person, unseen during training. The method requires a short ($\sim3$ seconds) sample of the target person, and the steering of the generation is done at inference time without any training steps. At the heart of the method lies a sampling process that combines the estimation of the denoising model together with a low-pass version of the new speaker's sample. Objective and subjective evaluation shows that our sampling method is able to generate a voice similar to target speaker in the frequency domain, on a level that is comparable with state of the art methods and without training.
